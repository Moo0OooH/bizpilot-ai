## 1. Scope Guard

Phase 18 is founder-led validation for cleaning businesses only.

Do not sell or build booking, invoices, calendar sync, SMS or WhatsApp automation, AI auto-send, autonomous AI operator behavior, a full CRM, marketplace features, a second vertical, or a mobile app.

The demo promise stays:

```text
Public quote link -> organized lead -> missing details -> owner-reviewed reply draft -> manual owner follow-up.
```

## 2. Founder CRM Workflow

Use `artifacts/phase18/BizPilot_Phase18_Founder_CRM_Template.xlsx` as the operating tracker until real pilot usage proves a need for more tooling.

For each prospect, fill:

- business name, city, website/social links, owner or contact name;
- contact channel and first outreach date;
- current quote process in the owner's words;
- current tools, likely response gaps, and public link placement ideas;
- fit score: `Strong fit`, `Good fit`, `Maybe`, or `Not now`;
- demo status, pilot status, next action, follow-up date;
- exact objections, exact owner wording, and willingness-to-pay notes.

Do not contact clear bad fits: large franchises, businesses with no incoming leads, owners who require dispatch/booking, owners who demand auto-messaging, or businesses unwilling to place a public quote link.

## 3. Weekly Outreach Loop

Minimum weekly validation rhythm:

| Metric | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Personalized outreach attempts | 5 |
| Meaningful replies or conversations | 2 |
| Demo attempts | 1 |
| CRM hygiene | every reply, objection, and next action logged |

Use the same positioning every time:

```text
You do not need a full CRM yet.
You need to stop losing cleaning quote requests.
```

Default cold outreach:

```text
Hi [Name], I saw your cleaning business and noticed customers probably ask for quotes through DMs, texts, or short website messages.

I am testing BizPilot, a done-for-you quote recovery system for cleaning businesses.

It gives you a branded quote link, collects cleaner customer details, and prepares reply/follow-up drafts. Nothing sends automatically; you stay in control.

I can make a quick demo using your business name and services if you want to see it.
```

## 4. Demo Flow

Use one repeatable demo story:

1. Start with the messy quote request problem: "How much for cleaning?"
2. Open the branded public quote link.
3. Submit or show a structured cleaning quote request.
4. Open `/dashboard` and show the urgent lead, quote link, and next action.
5. Open `/dashboard/leads/[leadId]`.
6. Show captured details, missing information, recommended action, and AI draft area.
7. Say: "BizPilot prepares the draft. You review, copy, and send it yourself."
8. Close on faster replies, fewer forgotten quote requests, and better manual follow-up.

Do not describe BizPilot as an AI operating system, autonomous agent, booking product, messaging automation tool, or full CRM.

## 5. Pilot Offer Decision

The GTM playbook recommends:

| Offer | Setup | Monthly | Notes |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Founder Setup | `$199` | `$49/mo` | Lower-friction early pilot offer. |
| Founder Plus | `$299` | `$79/mo` | Recommended default in GTM playbook. |

Owner still needs to decide the actual pilot offer before paid outreach:

- pilot price;
- setup fee or no setup fee;
- trial length, if any;
- cancellation and refund wording;
- whether the first three pilots receive founder pricing permanently or for a fixed term.

Until approved, use this non-binding wording:

```text
I am offering founder-led setup for the first few cleaning businesses. I will confirm the exact pilot price before you decide, and you can cancel if it is not helping after setup.
```

## 6. Objection And Owner Wording Tracking

Record exact owner language, not cleaned-up summaries. The wording is product evidence.

Track these categories:

| Category | What to capture |
| --- | --- |
| Current process | How requests arrive today: DMs, phone, website form, Google, Facebook, Instagram. |
| Pain language | "I forget", "I reply late", "people ghost", "they ask how much", "I am too busy". |
| Trust concern | Any discomfort about AI, privacy, customer data, or message automation. |
| Pricing reaction | Too expensive, reasonable, wants trial, asks about setup fee, asks about cancellation. |
| Feature pull | Booking, SMS, WhatsApp, invoices, CRM, calendar, payment, mobile app. Log only; do not build. |
| Value statement | Any sentence that proves quote recovery matters enough to pay for. |

Default objection responses:

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "I already use messages." | "That is exactly why this helps. BizPilot does not replace your messages; it gives customers a better quote link first so you receive cleaner details and can reply faster." |
| "I do not want AI talking to customers." | "It does not. BizPilot prepares drafts only. You decide what to send." |
| "I do not need a CRM." | "BizPilot is not a CRM. It helps before a CRM usually matters: quote request, missing details, reply draft, follow-up action." |
| "I am too busy to set it up." | "The pilot is founder-led. I set up the quote link, services, and first workflow with you." |
| "We already have a website form." | "Most forms only collect data. BizPilot turns the request into an owner-ready lead with missing info, urgency, reply draft, follow-up draft, and next action." |

## 7. First-Customer Setup

For any accepted pilot:

1. Confirm cleaning-business fit.
2. Configure business name, slug, services, service areas, colors, and logo URL if available.
3. Configure quote questions and consent wording.
4. Place the quote link in at least two channels.
5. Submit one demo-safe quote request.
6. Verify the lead appears in the dashboard.
7. Show the owner the manual AI draft workflow.
8. Schedule the first weekly check-in.
9. Record willingness to pay and success criteria.

## 8. Expansion Lock

Only revisit new scope after:

- 3 cleaning businesses are paying or payment-ready;
- each has at least 2 public quote link placements;
- real quote submissions are coming in;
- owners review leads weekly;
- owners copy or edit drafts manually;
- at least one owner gives a strong value statement;
- retention looks healthy after onboarding.
