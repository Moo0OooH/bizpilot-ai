# BizPilot AI - Phase 18 Pilot Operating Guide v1.0

## Purpose

This guide starts Phase 18 without expanding product scope. Phase 18 is founder-led validation with three cleaning businesses, using the existing Quote Recovery Command Center.

## Phase 18 Rule

Do not build new product features during pilot setup unless a safety, RLS, public quote, or customer-trust blocker is found.

Log requests for booking, billing, SMS, WhatsApp, auto-send, second verticals, or full CRM behavior as customer discovery notes.

## Before Outreach

Complete these steps manually for each prospect:

1. Open the Founder CRM workbook.
2. Add one row in `Founder CRM`.
3. Fill business name, owner/contact, city, website, social profile, and contact channel.
4. Rate fit as `Strong fit`, `Good fit`, `Maybe`, or `Not now`.
5. Write the current quote process in plain language.
6. Add at least one public quote link placement idea.
7. Set `Demo status` to `Not contacted`.
8. Set `Pilot status` to `Prospect`.
9. Set the next action.

Do not contact a business that is a clear bad fit: large franchise, no incoming leads, needs dispatch/booking, demands auto-messaging, or will not place a quote link.

## Outreach Message

Lead with quote recovery, not AI software:

```text
Hi [Name], I saw your cleaning business and noticed customers probably ask for quotes through DMs, texts, or short website messages.

I am testing BizPilot, a done-for-you quote recovery system for cleaning businesses.

It gives you a branded quote link, collects cleaner customer details, and prepares reply/follow-up drafts. Nothing sends automatically; you stay in control.

I can make a quick demo using your business name and services if you want to see it.
```

Record the exact owner reply in the workbook.

## Demo Flow

Use the same story every time:

1. Show the messy quote request problem.
2. Open the public quote link.
3. Submit or show a structured cleaning quote request.
4. Open the dashboard and show the Magic Moment.
5. Open the lead detail.
6. Show the AI summary and reply draft.
7. Say clearly: BizPilot prepares the draft; the owner reviews, copies, and sends manually.
8. Close with: faster replies, fewer forgotten quote requests, better follow-up.

Do not say BizPilot is a full CRM, booking system, automation suite, or AI operator.

## Concierge Setup Checklist

For each pilot customer, complete the `Concierge Setup` sheet:

1. Confirm cleaning business fit.
2. Collect services and service areas.
3. Configure business name, slug, colors, and logo URL if available.
4. Configure quote questions.
5. Place the public quote link in at least two channels.
6. Submit one test quote request.
7. Verify the lead appears in the dashboard.
8. Generate or show the owner-reviewed draft.
9. Schedule the first weekly check-in.
10. Record willingness to pay.

## Weekly Pilot Check-In

Every week, record:

- real quote submissions,
- public link placements,
- owner-reviewed leads,
- AI drafts generated,
- AI drafts copied or edited,
- follow-ups completed,
- booked jobs influenced,
- owner value statement,
- friction or churn risk,
- requested features.

If the owner is not using BizPilot, diagnose before building:

- Is the quote link not placed?
- Are there no incoming leads?
- Is the owner unclear on the workflow?
- Are AI drafts low quality?
- Is the customer a bad ICP fit?
- Is pricing the blocker?

## Phase 18 Success Criteria

Phase 18 is working when:

- 3 cleaning businesses are paying or payment-ready.
- Each has at least 2 public quote link placements.
- Real quote submissions are coming in.
- Owners review leads weekly.
- AI drafts are copied or edited.
- At least one owner gives a strong value statement.
- Retention looks healthy after onboarding.

## Expansion Lock

Do not start Phase 8 expansion, second verticals, booking, billing automation, direct messaging automation, or full CRM work until the validation gate is met.
